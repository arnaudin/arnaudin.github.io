I'd recommend _The Shape of Design_ to any designer, and to anyone curious about design. It isn't a practical how-to; it's a short, philosophical read about *why* we make things, delivered through witty, metaphorical meditation. A few sections tip into the self-indulgent, but it's a quick read that's guaranteed to make you think deeper about the work.

A few highlights that stuck with me:

> To be human is to tinker, to envision a better condition, and decide to work toward it by shaping the world around us.

> The Shakers have a proverb that says, “Do not make something unless it is both necessary and useful; but if it is both, do not hesitate to make it beautiful.”

> The world shapes us, and we get to shape the world.

That last line is the one I keep coming back to. It's a big part of why I've spent my career building tools for other creators: the things we make don't just solve a problem in front of us, they change what the people using them can imagine next. Chimero frames design not as a field of artifacts but of *outcomes and consequences*, a reframe that has quietly shaped how I think about the products I work on.

Get it free at *[The Shape of Design](http://www.shapeofdesignbook.com/)*.
